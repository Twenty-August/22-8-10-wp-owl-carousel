Millennium Tower, Boston

Developed by

Fisher Brothers / Witkoff Group

Designed by

KPF/ Rockwell Group

MEP Engineer

Derive Engineers

This soaring 60-story tower at the Franklin Street site will be Boston’s fourth tallest building. It will connect at the lower levels to the historic Burnham Building, creating a unique blend of past and future in true Bostonian tradition.

The tower will feature nearly 500 luxury condominiums. On the lower 3 floors of both the Tower and the Burnham building, up to 196,000 sq. ft. of prime retail space will house some of the hottest boutique and flagship retail in Boston. This luxury project in a formerly neglected section of downtown Boston has sparked citywide excitement and will attract vibrant new retail and investment back to the area.

1 Willoughby Square, Brooklyn, NY

Developed by

JEMB Realty

Designed by

FXCollaborative

MEP Engineer

Cosentini

1 Willoughby Square is Brooklyn’s first ground-up office tower in a generation. 1WSQ opens directly onto the 1-acre Willoughby Square Park and rises 34 stories above Brooklyn to offer unobstructed views of New York City skylines and waterways.

The 34-story, 500,000-square-foot structure is also Brooklyn’s tallest office tower. 1WSQ features private outdoor spaces on multiple floors, a 250-space bicycle parking area, advanced air filtration and operable windows to allow fresh air to circulate within the building. 1 Willoughby Square is set to become Brooklyn’s most distinctive and sought-after business address, the perfect building for a post-pandemic world, providing the latest in touchless technology. The project is set for completion in the second quarter of 2021.

Designed by

KPF/ Rockwell Group

MEP Engineer

Derive Engineers

Designed by the renowned Robert A.M. Stern Architects, the elegant limestone tower combines a 186-room hotel with 143 luxury condominiums known as the Four Seasons Private Residences New York at 30 Park Place.

The Four Seasons Hotel New York, Downtown will occupy the first 22 floors of the 80-story tower, which will also include a specialty restaurant operated by the Four Seasons. The remainder of the tower, which rises to a dramatic skyline profile of full-floor penthouses and setback terraces, will accommodate private residences as large as 6,500 square feet. The Residences also will be managed by the Four Seasons.

Feller Engineering

On South Florida’s glorious Atlantic coastline, just 30 minutes north of Miami and 45 minutes south of Palm Beach, the Four Seasons Hotel and Private Residences Fort Lauderdale is the centerpiece, and the newest addition, to the Four Seasons Fort Collection.
